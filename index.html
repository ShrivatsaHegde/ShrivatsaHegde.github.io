Summer Internship

November 2022 - February 2023

Identification of Focal Adhesion Kinase as a target for anticancer activity of Terminalia elliptica using computational approaches

This study focused on exploring the therapeutic potential of natural products in breast cancer treatment, specifically utilizing Terminalia elliptica, a traditional plant known for its various medicinal properties.

By screening phytobioactives present in Terminalia elliptica using molecular docking, the research identified chebulagic Acid as a promising lead molecule with superior interaction and binding affinity to Focal Adhesion Kinase (FAK) compared to the standard drug podophyllotoxin.

Acquired hands-on experience in advanced computational techniques, including protein-ligand docking, virtual screening, and binding affinity predictions, to identify and evaluate potential drug candidates.
